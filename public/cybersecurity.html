What is Cybersecurity?

Cybersecurity is the practice of protecting systems, networks, and programs from digital attacks. These cyberattacks are usually aimed at accessing, changing, or destroying sensitive information.

  • Protects personal and business data
  • Prevents financial losses from attacks
  • Maintains privacy and confidentiality
  • Ensures system availability and integrity

Common Cyber Threats

Viruses & Malware

Malicious software designed to damage systems

Prevention Tips:

  • Use reliable antivirus software
  • Avoid suspicious downloads
  • Keep software updated
  • Scan email attachments

Phishing

Fraudulent attempts to obtain sensitive information

Red Flags:

  • Urgent or threatening language
  • Suspicious sender addresses
  • Generic greetings
  • Unexpected attachments

Password Cracking

Unauthorized access through weak passwords

Protection:

  • Use complex passwords
  • Enable two-factor authentication
  • Use password managers
  • Change passwords regularly

Social Engineering

Psychological manipulation to divulge information

Warning Signs:

  • Unsolicited contact
  • Pressure tactics
  • Too-good-to-be-true offers
  • Requests for sensitive info

Cybersecurity Best Practices

1

Use Strong, Unique Passwords

Create complex passwords with a mix of letters, numbers, and symbols. Use different passwords for each account.

2

Enable Two-Factor Authentication

Add an extra layer of security by requiring a second form of verification beyond your password.

3

Avoid Suspicious Links

Don't click on links in unexpected emails or messages. Verify the sender before taking action.

4

Keep Software Updated

Install security updates promptly to protect against known vulnerabilities.

5

Backup Your Data Regularly

Create regular backups of important data to protect against ransomware and data loss.
